## Customer Onboarding Architecture
# Universal Loan Customer Onboarding Architecture

## Executive Summary

This case study explores how a fragmented lending onboarding process can be redesigned as an end-to-end Customer and Operations capability.

The objective was to improve onboarding quality, operational efficiency, banker enablement, compliance readiness, and servicing outcomes by aligning customer journeys, operational workflows, Salesforce capabilities, fulfilment controls, and governance processes into a single target-state architecture.

The solution demonstrates domain architecture thinking across Customer, Operations, Risk & Compliance, Core Banking, and Salesforce enablement domains.

## Key Architecture Decisions

| Decision | Reason |
|-----------|---------|
| Use Salesforce Case Management as onboarding entry point | Reuse existing platform capability and reduce implementation cost |
| Surface knowledge within the banker workflow | Reduce dependency on tribal knowledge |
| Introduce eligibility guidance before case progression | Improve fit-for-processing quality |
| Separate guidance from training | Keep operational workflows lightweight |
| Retain tactical low-code approach | Funding constraints and faster time-to-value |

## Architecture Impact

The proposed architecture creates:

- Earlier eligibility clarity
- Reduced fulfilment rework
- Improved banker enablement
- Better process visibility
- Stronger compliance controls
- Increased servicing readiness
- Foundation for future automation and AI enablement
