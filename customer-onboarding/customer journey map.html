Purpose

Why this journey map matters.

Universal Loan onboarding is not only a front-end experience. It is a connected journey across customer intent, banker guidance, eligibility checks, document readiness, compliance, fulfilment validation, and post-onboarding servicing.

Customer Intent Banker Discovery Eligibility Documents Fulfilment Servicing
Journey Map

End-to-end customer onboarding journey.

01

Customer Need

Customer expresses a lending need or funding requirement through a banker, relationship channel, or banking engagement.

  • Customer wants clarity on options
  • Banker needs to identify product fit
  • Early expectation-setting is critical
02

Banker Discovery

Banker explores customer needs, product suitability, collateral position, relationship context, and initial eligibility indicators.

  • Clarify lending purpose
  • Confirm relationship and segment fit
  • Introduce Universal Loan requirements
03

Eligibility Guidance

Eligibility guidance helps prevent weak submissions by surfacing key rules before the journey moves into fulfilment.

  • Check collateral readiness
  • Confirm consent and KYC/FICA readiness
  • Identify exceptions early
04

Document Request

Customer is guided on the supporting documents required for assessment, validation, and fulfilment processing.

  • Reduce unclear document requests
  • Improve completeness before submission
  • Limit back-and-forth with operations
05

Case Creation

Salesforce case management becomes the operational anchor for visibility, routing, documentation, and workflow tracking.

  • Create structured onboarding case
  • Apply correct product and service type
  • Enable status and ownership visibility
06

Sales Preparation

Sales preparation packages the customer request into a fulfilment-ready submission with the required business, credit, and operational information.

  • Complete forms and supporting artefacts
  • Validate completeness
  • Prepare for fulfilment review
07

Fulfilment Validation

Fulfilment validates the submission against operational rules, facility limits, security status, risk checks, and approval requirements.

  • Run validation checks
  • Identify exceptions
  • Escalate where required
08

Servicing Readiness

Once approved and activated, the customer journey transitions into ongoing servicing, relationship management, and operational support.

  • Confirm customer outcome
  • Update case status
  • Enable future servicing visibility
Journey Insight

The customer experiences one journey, but the bank executes many operating steps.

The architecture challenge is making those internal steps visible, governed, coordinated, and service-ready without making the customer experience feel fragmented.

Design Considerations

What the journey needed to solve.

Customer Clarity

Customers need to understand what is required, what happens next, and why certain documents or validations are needed.

Banker Enablement

Bankers need embedded guidance, product knowledge, eligibility prompts, and structured workflow support.

Operational Quality

Operations teams need complete, accurate, fit-for-processing submissions to reduce rework and fulfilment delays.

Servicing Continuity

The onboarding journey must create a clean handover into long-term customer servicing and relationship management.
