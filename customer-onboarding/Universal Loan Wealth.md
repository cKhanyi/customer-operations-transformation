# Customer Onboarding Architecture  
## Universal Loan for Wealth Clients

## Architecture Question

How do we onboard a customer across banking channels while maintaining compliance, servicing readiness, operational efficiency, and fulfilment quality?

## Case Study Context

Universal Loan onboarding was not only a customer journey problem. It was a lending operating model challenge involving product rules, collateral requirements, banker guidance, fulfilment controls, Salesforce case architecture, knowledge governance, and tactical delivery constraints.

The project explored how the Universal Loan / Universal Offering process could move onto Salesforce Customer One using a tactical, low-code approach while preserving future scalability.

## Core Problem

The onboarding journey was fragmented across:

- Banker product knowledge
- Manual One Pager completion
- Instalment calculator dependency
- Document collection
- Sales Support validation
- Fulfilment checks
- Credit and risk exceptions
- Legacy system validation
- Salesforce case routing

This created slow turnaround times, inconsistent guidance, rework, and limited visibility across the journey.

## Architecture Position

The solution treats onboarding as an end-to-end banking capability rather than a form, screen, or loan process.

Customer intent must connect to:

- Product guidance
- Eligibility screening
- KYC/FICA readiness
- Collateral validation
- Case creation
- Document collection
- Sales preparation
- Fulfilment validation
- Credit approval
- Servicing readiness

## Key Takeaway

Universal Loan onboarding is a lending operating model problem disguised as a customer experience problem. The architecture challenge is to connect product rules, banker behaviour, fulfilment controls, compliance requirements, case architecture, and knowledge governance into one coherent onboarding capability.
